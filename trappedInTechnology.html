Wireframes

I want to create a satirical website that informs users of the tactics employed by social networking and video platform sites to get users hooked. This information is conveyed through interacting with the website's elements.

sketch 1: desktop

Stayhere

The layout is modelled after Netflix’s website. The website’s name is “Stayhere.” While the user navigates on Stayhere, a Facebook notification will sporadically pop up on the screen, simulating real-life notifications and intermittent schedules.

Note: Instead of Facebook, I will call the parody website "Facelock." The change in the name will be reflected on the final website.

sketch 2: desktop

Facelock

If the user clicks on the notification, it will take them to a Facelock page. The user scrolls down the page to view a variety of posts, ranging from ads to friends' posts to events.

The ads address physical and mental health problems connected to excessive technology use. For instance, there will be an ad for eye drops that cure dry eyes from extended blue-light exposure.

The friends' posts serve as filler content. Sometimes the user will see something exciting (maybe a cute animal), and sometimes the user will see something completely irrelevant or boring. Once again, this is to simulate an intermittent reinforcement schedule.

Facelock will feature events that encourage people to spend more time on screens (e.g. "Winter BingeFest! Watch all x seasons of [insert TV show name] with your friends online!")
